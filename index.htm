Aimed at our undergraduate art students, this guide will provide resources and help in accessing these resources.  For a look at the Art and Art History Library Guides already written by our resident subject expert Jessica Barbera, check the link below.
Websites
LibGuides
The Art and Art History subjects that can be found through the Hoover Library are available in a wide variety of media and forms.  Through the various search tools offered, one can find artists by name or by work.  If books are more your speed, the library offers an extensive selection, call numbers listed below.  Don't feel constrained to limit yourself to the books within the physical library either, as you can find a variety of eBooks available through the Hoover Library.  If you're not sure how to find materials in the library, an online tutorial video is available and our friendly staff will welcome questions and get you to where you need to go.  When searching for articles, there are 10 databases to choose from, including JSTOR, Academic Search Complete, and Oxford Art Online.  Finding images is simple as well, as Hoover Library boasts 5 FREE resources as well as the highly recommended ARTstor.  Finally, there are streaming video services available through Hoover Library, DVDs on premises, and two seperate free resources for video art all at your fingertips.
